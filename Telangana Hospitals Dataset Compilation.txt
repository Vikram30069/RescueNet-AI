RescueNet AI Emergency Response
Architecture: Telangana State Hospital
Infrastructure Analysis and Verified
Dataset
The deployment of autonomous algorithmic dispatch systems requires highly deterministic,
rigorously verified foundational datasets. RescueNet AI, designed to orchestrate emergency
medical responses, fundamentally depends on the spatial accuracy, operational readiness, and
communication reliability of terminal nodes—in this context, tertiary and quaternary care
hospitals. This report delivers an exhaustive, production-ready dataset of the hospital network
across the state of Telangana, India. The analysis evaluates the profound structural evolution of
Telangana’s healthcare architecture, detailing the transition from historical urban centralization
toward a distributed, district-level medical ecosystem.
The evaluation meticulously covers the priority target districts of Hyderabad, Secunderabad,
Sangareddy, Warangal, Nizamabad, Karimnagar, and Khammam, while expanding into the
broader geographic expanse of the state to ensure an unbroken emergency safety net. By
analyzing governmental registries, directorate directories, and institutional operational data,
this intelligence package provides the algorithmic parameters necessary for RescueNet AI to
execute intelligent, acuity-based triage and routing protocols.
Analytical Framework and Dataset Objectives
Constructing an emergency response dataset transcends rudimentary web scraping; it
necessitates the establishment of a rigid verification hierarchy to eliminate the catastrophic
risks associated with phantom nodes, incorrect geolocations, or dead communication lines
during acute crises. The dataset formulation adhered to strict data collection rules, prioritizing
primary governmental sources over unverified aggregators.
The primary tier of verification utilized the Directorate of Medical Education (DME), the
Telangana Vaidhya Vidhana Parishad (TVVP), and the Kaloji Narayana Rao University of Health
Sciences (KNRUHS) affiliation registries1. These portals provided the foundational legal and
operational status of each institution. The secondary tier involved cross-referencing individual
institutional platforms to extract specific geographic coordinates, Superintendent contact
numbers, and departmental capacities5. Tertiary verification leveraged standardized public
directories and health insurance network lists, such as the Aarogyasri network grids, to validate
alternate communication channels and confirm private sector integration8.
RescueNet AI relies on a tripartite confidence scoring mechanism to assign routing
probabilities. A "HIGH" confidence score indicates that a facility’s name, address,
geocoordinates, and at least one communication line were verified via primary governmental
or institutional documentation. A "MEDIUM" score indicates partial reliance on secondary
directories or instances where a dedicated institutional website is absent or under
construction, though the physical presence and primary phone lines are confirmed through
state health bulletins. The dataset strictly excludes any "LOW" confidence or unverified entries
from the final routing matrix to prevent dispatch errors.
The Urban Core Ecosystem: Hyderabad and
Secunderabad
The capital region of Telangana constitutes a hyper-dense urban agglomeration that
historically monopolized the state's advanced medical infrastructure. RescueNet AI dispatch
logic in this zone must account for high casualty volumes, severe traffic latency, and the
specific specialized competencies of legacy institutions.
Apex Institutions of Hyderabad
Hyderabad houses institutions that serve as the ultimate referral destinations for complex,
multi-system trauma and rare acute pathologies. Osmania General Hospital represents the
historical and operational anchor of the southern healthcare grid. Founded in 1910 under the
patronage of Mir Osman Ali Khan at a historic expenditure of ₹2,00,00,000, the institution has
evolved to maintain a massive 1,168-bed capacity10. Situated in Afzal Gunj along the Musi River,
Osmania General Hospital handles extreme outpatient and emergency volumes, making it the
primary routing destination for mass casualty incidents in the old city10.
Operating as an autonomous state university, the Nizam's Institute of Medical Sciences (NIMS)
in Punjagutta serves as a critical node for advanced neurological, cardiothoracic, and
oncological emergencies12. The institution operates specialized blocks, including dedicated
oncology and emergency trauma centers, necessitating that RescueNet AI algorithms prioritize
NIMS for cases requiring immediate neurosurgical or cardiovascular interventions14.
For pediatric and obstetric emergencies, specialized routing is mandatory. Niloufer Hospital for
Women and Children, located in the Red Hills district, is one of the largest pediatric facilities in
Asia15. Operating with a 500-bed capacity, it provides advanced neonatal intensive care and
emergency maternal surgery16. General trauma algorithms must automatically divert acute
pediatric cases to this node rather than generalized trauma centers to optimize survival
outcomes. Furthermore, the Sir Ronald Ross Institute of Tropical and Communicable Diseases,
commonly known as the Fever Hospital, remains the state’s primary isolation and treatment
center during severe infectious disease outbreaks, representing a highly specific routing
parameter for epidemiological emergencies18.
The Secunderabad Cantonment Network
The twin city of Secunderabad operates an independent but deeply integrated healthcare
network. The centerpiece is Gandhi Medical College and Hospital. Originating as a modest
three-ward cantonment infirmary in 1851, the facility has expanded into a 1,200-bed apex
teaching hospital19. Gandhi Hospital operates as the primary government trauma intercept for
the northern sectors of the capital region, effectively balancing the load with Osmania General
Hospital.
To supplement the public infrastructure, corporate entities enrolled in the state’s Aarogyasri
health insurance scheme provide critical surge capacity. Krishna Institute of Medical Sciences
(KIMS) in Secunderabad offers high-acuity intensive care and rapid intervention capabilities,
verified as a pivotal node for complex medical emergencies where public infrastructure may be
saturated21.

Priority Tier-2 Regional Hubs
The geographic expanse of the Deccan Plateau renders direct medevac to Hyderabad clinically
unviable for time-sensitive traumas occurring in the hinterlands. Between 2021 and 2024, the
state government aggressively executed a decentralization policy, upgrading district-level
hospitals into full-fledged teaching institutions with massive bed capacities23. RescueNet AI
must treat these tier-2 hubs as independent primary nodes, capable of sustaining critically ill
patients without immediate reliance on the capital.
The Eastern Anchor: Warangal
Warangal functions as the definitive medical capital for eastern Telangana. The Mahatma
Gandhi Memorial (MGM) Hospital dominates the regional architecture, boasting an
extraordinary 1,450-bed capacity and managing approximately 1,800 outpatients daily6.
Situated near the Kakatiya Medical College, MGM Hospital intercepts agricultural trauma,
industrial accidents, and acute medical crises from surrounding rural mandals.
To optimize triage, Warangal’s infrastructure is segmented. The CKM Government Maternity
Hospital operates distinctly to handle obstetric emergencies, while the Regional Eye Hospital
manages severe ocular trauma27. This segmentation requires RescueNet AI to parse the exact
nature of the emergency and execute precise intra-city routing, preventing the generalized
MGM emergency room from being overwhelmed by cases better suited for the specialized
peripheral institutes.
The Northern Corridors: Nizamabad and Karimnagar
Nizamabad, located near the Maharashtra state border, serves a highly transient and
cross-border demographic. The Government General Hospital in Khaleelwadi plays a pivotal
role in regional health delivery, managing high-velocity traumas originating from the
surrounding national highways28. The integration of established private networks, such as
Pragathi Hospital, further augments the district's intensive care bandwidth under the
Aarogyasri scheme9.
In Karimnagar, the establishment of a new Government Medical College in 2023 on a 25-acre
campus has transformed regional capabilities32. The attached Government General Hospital
currently operates a 350-bed facility that intercepts patients who previously faced extensive
transit times32. This public infrastructure is supported by the Chalmeda Anand Rao Institute of
Medical Sciences (CAIMS), ensuring substantial redundancy in emergency medical
bandwidth34.
The Western and South-Eastern Frontiers: Sangareddy and Khammam
Sangareddy represents a rapidly industrializing corridor connecting Telangana to Karnataka
and Maharashtra. The Government General Hospital, situated near the district collectorate,
provides immediate stabilization for industrial and transit-related casualties35. The MNR Medical
College and Hospital in Fasalwadi enhances the district's capacity with state-of-the-art
diagnostic and surgical infrastructure37.
Khammam anchors the south-eastern frontier, bordering dense forested and tribal belts. The
Government General Hospital in Khammam has undergone profound upgrades, now equipped
with 750 beds3. Crucially, this includes a dedicated 150-bed Mother and Child Hospital (MCH)
block, a 20-bed dialysis ward, and an advanced Cardiac, Emergency, and Trauma Care Centre3.
Supported by the Mamata General and Super Speciality Hospital, Khammam operates as a
highly autonomous medical zone, drastically reducing mortality rates for patients originating
from remote eastern mandals24.

The Decentralization Frontier: Secondary District
Infrastructure
The success of a statewide AI response grid lies in its ability to map the deepest infrastructural
nodes. The "One District, One Medical College" initiative has rapidly populated the map with
robust secondary facilities, narrowing the radius of the critical "golden hour" for trauma and
cardiac emergencies.
In the extreme north, the Rajiv Gandhi Institute of Medical Sciences (RIMS) in Adilabad operates
a 500-bed facility that acts as a vital lifeline for tribal and border populations, effectively
eliminating a lethal transit dependency on Hyderabad40. Similarly, the newly sanctioned
Government Medical College in Mancherial operates a 350-bed teaching hospital serving the
coal-belt regions26.
The southern districts have seen parallel expansions. The Government Medical College in
Wanaparthy features a verified 605-bed capacity, fundamentally altering emergency logistics
in the southern agrarian belts43. Facilities in Mahabubnagar, Nalgonda, and Suryapet provide
continuous emergency coverage along the major southern transit routes connecting to Andhra
Pradesh44. RescueNet AI must continuously update the routing matrices for newly established
centers in districts like Jagtial, Bhadradri Kothagudem, and Jangaon, which have recently
transitioned from secondary area hospitals to primary teaching hospitals with advanced critical
care units47.
Specialized Networks and Sub-Routine Algorithmic
Dispatch
Beyond trauma and general medicine, the dataset reveals an extensive integration of
specialized care networks that require dedicated algorithmic sub-routines.
Palliative and Oncological Triage
Acute crises in terminally ill patients require highly specific interventions, particularly
concerning pain management and opioid availability. The state has embedded an extensive
palliative care network within its district hospital architecture. Facilities in Gadwal, Suryapet,
Kamareddy, Jangaon, and Wanaparthy house dedicated palliative care centers50. RescueNet AI
must map these specific nodes to ensure that palliative emergencies are routed to centers
verified to stock necessary analgesics and staffed by specialized pain management personnel,
minimizing unnecessary trauma to fragile patients.
The Aarogyasri Integration
The Rajiv Aarogyasri health insurance scheme effectively unifies public and corporate hospital
infrastructure. The dataset acknowledges that during mass casualty events or instances of
saturated public infrastructure, RescueNet AI can automatically divert economically
disadvantaged patients to verified corporate nodes—such as KIMS in Secunderabad or
Pragathi Hospital in Nizamabad—knowing that state funding mechanisms will cover the acute
intervention costs8.

Regional Density Summary
The spatial distribution of the dataset confirms a strong coverage footprint across 24 key
districts. The dataset includes 5 apex facilities in Hyderabad and 2 in Secunderabad, reflecting
urban density. Expanding outward, it successfully indexes multi-specialty hubs across 17 other
districts (including Warangal, Sangareddy, Nizamabad, Karimnagar, Khammam, Siddipet,
Medak, Adilabad, etc.).
Identification of Missing Geographies
While the dataset comprehensively covers the highest-acuity tier-1 and tier-2 facilities, certain
sparsely populated or newly formed districts rely heavily on secondary Community Health
Centers (CHCs). Districts currently absent from this apex tertiary care list include Kumuram

To continuously optimize the RescueNet AI logic, future iterations of this dataset should directly

State Blood Transfusion Council. Furthermore, scraping newly authorized construction
blueprints from the National Medical Commission (NMC) will allow the AI to proactively map
facilities slated for inauguration in the upcoming 2026-2027 fiscal cycles.
